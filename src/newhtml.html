Edinburgh E2

Date: Sat 31 Mar
Category: TALK
Event Type: Science Festival Events
Age: 14+
Price: £35 (Half day £20)

Scotland was home to the Enlightenment, but its role as a centre for ideas and creativity is not simply a historical one. The Edinburgh Enlightenment Exchange – aka Edinburgh E2 – brings together some of the country’s most creative individuals from the sciences, arts, creative industries, business and social enterprise for a day-long showcase.

Let’s Be Friends: Robot Companions

Date: Sun 01 Apr
Category: TALK
Event Type: Science Festival Events
Age: 14+
Price: £8 / £6

The press likes to scare us with wild tales of robots taking over the world. But in real life, researchers are trying to produce robots that fit comfortably into our lives: at home, at work, at play. Join Heriot Watt University’s Professor Ruth Aylett to hear about new work on human-robot interaction, see videos and a real robot, and put your questions to the experts.

Mysteries of the Human Genome

Date: Sun 01 Apr
Category: TALK
Event Type: Science Festival Events
Age: 14+
Price: £8 / £6

Dr Colin Semple, Head of Bioinformatics at the Medical Research Council Human Genetics Unit, knows that your genome is an amazing thing. It directed your journey from a fertilised egg to the person you are now, and our growing understanding of it promises to revolutionise our view of what it is to be human. Join him as he reveals why.

The Dancing Brain

Date: Sun 01 Apr
Category: TALK
Event Type: Science Festival Events
Age: 14+
Price: £8 / £6

The science of dance is fascinating. Did you know that the way you dance is influenced by your hormonal and genetic make-up, and that dancing in certain ways can make you think faster? Dr Peter Lovatt, a former professional dancer turned psychologist, aka Dr Dance, will have you dancing, laughing and learning as he leads you through the fascinating science of dance. Come and dance along as Peter reveals some fantastic facts about the science of dance, and shows you how dancing can help you think more creatively. Dancing shoes optional!

  • Book Now

History of Science Through Ten Objects

Date: Sun 01 Apr
Category: TALK
Event Type: Science Festival Events
Age: 14+
Price: £8 / £6

Inspired by Neil MacGregor’s award-wining series on the history of the world in 100 objects, physicist and author Manjit Kumar takes you on a journey through the history of science. Travel through time and across continents with him as he shares tales of discovery and wonder. Along the way, you’ll encounter some of the fascinating characters behind some of science’s most important and iconic objects and the revelations they enabled.

Build Your Own Time Machine

Date: Mon 02 Apr
Category: TALK
Event Type: Science Festival Events
Age: 14+
Price: £8 / £6

There is no physical law to prevent time travel — nothing in physics to say it is impossible. So surely the invention of a time machine is just a matter of ... well, time? At least according to Aclaimed science writer Brian Clegg. Join him as he shares some secrets – why wouldn’t HG Wells' time machine have worked. And what do you need to do to make a real one?
